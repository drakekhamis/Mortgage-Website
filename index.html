CONVENTIONAL LOANS

Fixed & adjustable-rate loans with flexible terms. The most common mortgage product in America.

Down payment from 3%
15, 20, or 30-year terms
Fixed or adjustable rate
No upfront mortgage insurance
Cancel PMI at 80% LTV
Loans up to $766,550

Conventional loans are backed by Fannie Mae or Freddie Mac and offer the widest range of terms and flexibility. With good credit and a reasonable down payment, this is often the most cost-effective long-term choice. Unlike FHA, there's no upfront MIP and PMI can be removed once you have 20% equity.

FHA LOANS

Government-backed loans designed for buyers with lower credit scores or smaller down payments.

Down payment from 3.5%
Credit scores from 580
Gift funds allowed
Higher DTI ratios accepted
FHA 203k rehab available
Streamline refinance option

FHA loans are insured by the Federal Housing Administration and are perfect for first-time homebuyers or those rebuilding credit. The lower down payment requirement and flexible credit standards make homeownership accessible when conventional financing may be out of reach. FHA loans come with both upfront and annual mortgage insurance premiums.

Zero down payment required

VA home loans are one of the most powerful benefits available to those who have served. Loan amounts $766,551+

Jumbo loans are required when your loan amount exceeds the Fannie Mae/Freddie Mac conforming limit of $766,550. These loans have stricter qualification requirements including higher credit scores, lower DTI ratios, and larger reserves — but they allow you to finance the luxury Arizona properties that conventional programs can't cover.

USDA LOANS

100% financing for eligible rural and suburban properties in designated USDA zones.

Zero down payment
Low mortgage insurance rates
Competitive interest rates
Eligible suburban areas in AZ

USDA Rural Development loans offer 100% financing with no down payment for eligible properties in designated rural and suburban areas. Many parts of greater Phoenix qualify — including areas in Maricopa and Pinal counties. USDA loans have income limits and property eligibility requirements, but for those who qualify, they represent one of the best financing options available.

HELOC

A revolving line of credit secured by your home equity. Use it when you need it.

Access up to 90% CLTV
Revolving draw period
Interest-only payment option
Variable or fixed rate options
Use for any purpose
Low closing costs

A Home Equity Line of Credit (HELOC) gives you access to a revolving credit line based on your home's equity. It's ideal for home improvements, education costs, or as an emergency fund. You draw what you need, when you need it, and only pay interest on what you've used. Arizona homeowners who've seen strong appreciation in recent years are in an excellent position to leverage their equity.
